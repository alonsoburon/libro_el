Framework 13
Load strategy spectrum
Ordered by cost and complexity. The corridor determines where each fits.
CHEAP · SIMPLE
EXPENSIVE · COMPLEX
1
Append-only
Pure INSERT. No key matching. Idempotent at view level, not table level.
COST
2
Full replace
Staging swap or partition swap. No merge logic. Idempotent by construction.
COST
3
Append + materialize
Append raw + dedup view. Shifts cost from load-time to read-time.
COST
4
Merge / upsert
Match on key, update or insert. Most expensive DML in columnar engines.
COST
5
Hybrid append + merge
Two destinations (append log + merged current). Maximum complexity.
COST
Corridor effect
In T→T, MERGE is cheap -- the spectrum compresses to positions 1-2-4. In T→C, the full spread matters and position 3 earns its place.
